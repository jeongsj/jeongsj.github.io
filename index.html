Seong-Jo Jeong

정성조·Postdoctoral Researcher·Ph.D. in Sociology

I am a sociologist of social movements, politics, and inequality, working with South Korea's LGBTQ communities as my primary field. My research asks three broader questions: how marginalized movements build solidarity and contend with anti-gender politics over democracy; how discrimination and violence at work, in institutions, and in everyday life turn into inequalities in health and labor; and how knowledge about gender and sexuality is produced, sustained, and contested. Working across qualitative, quantitative, and mixed methods, I also practice public sociology, translating research into essays, reports, and public talks.

Research

I.

Queer Democracy — Movement & Politics

How movements build coalitions and reshape democratic life. My dissertation traces the trajectory of the Korean LGBTQ movement through its hegemonic struggle with anti-gender politics, linking movement formation to the politics of solidarity and anti-discrimination law.

Projects & publications→
II.

Health, Discrimination & Inequality

Quantitative and mixed-methods studies of discrimination and inequality. For sexual and gender minorities, I trace coming out, minority stress, and mental health. More broadly, I also examine sexual violence and discrimination at work and in institutions.

Projects & publications→
III.

Queer Studies as a Field

How an academic field is made and sustained. Meta-research on queer studies in South Korea, documenting its bibliographic record, examining researchers' working conditions, and studying the institutional infrastructure that shapes what can be studied and by whom.
