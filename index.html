About Me

Hello. I am a Ph.D. Candidate in the Department of Sociology at Chung-Ang University, South Korea. I specialize in the sociology of social movements, gender, and sexuality.

Currently, I am working on my doctoral dissertation titled "Queer Democracy: The Formation of LGBTQ Movement and Anti-Discrimination Project in South Korea." In this project, I investigate the historical trajectory of the Korean LGBTQ movement, analyzing how marginalized groups have expanded the horizon of democracy through solidarity politics and the struggle for anti-discrimination laws.

As a researcher, I employ a mixed-methods approach, conducting quantitative research on health disparities and discrimination within the LGBTQ community alongside my qualitative work. Beyond academic inquiry, I am committed to public sociology. I actively foster a queer researcher community and engage with the public through essays and policy reports on critical social issues. By translating sociological insights into public discourse, I aim to bridge the gap between research and activism to contribute to social change.
